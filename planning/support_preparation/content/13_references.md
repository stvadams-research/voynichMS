# 13. References and Technical Baselines

This research acknowledges the following data sources, methodological foundations, and comparative artifacts utilized within the Assumption-Resistant Framework.

## 13.1 Primary Data and Transliteration

**Zandbergen-Landini Transliteration (Interlinear Archive)**  
Zandbergen, R., & Landini, G. (2024). *The Voynich Manuscript Transliteration Archive*.  
Source of the primary digital ledger used in Phase 1-9. 

**Extensible Voynich Alphabet (EVA)**  
Guy, J. B. (1991). *The Extensible Voynich Alphabet (EVA)*.  
Adopted as the canonical tokenization backbone for all text-stream analysis.

## 13.2 Methodological Foundations

**Information Theory and Clustering**  
Montemurro, M. A., & Zanette, D. H. (2013). "Keywords and Co-occurrence Patterns in the Voynich Manuscript: An Information-Theoretic Analysis." *PLoS ONE*, 8(6), e66319.  
The basis for the information-clustering and keyword-stability metrics evaluated in Phase 4.

**The Hoax Hypothesis and Table-Grille Generation**  
Rugg, G. (2004). "The Voynich Manuscript: An Elegant Hoax?" *Cryptologia*, 28(1), 31-46.  
Primary source for the "Rugg-style" table-and-grille generative models used as test targets in Phase 2 and 5.

**Self-Citation and Statistical Evidence**  
Schinner, A. (2007). "The Voynich Manuscript: Evidence of the Hoax Hypothesis." *Cryptologia*, 31(2), 95-107.  
Methodological basis for the self-citation and repetition-rate baselines tested in Phase 3.

**Timm's Generative Models**  
Timm, T. (2014). "The Use of Self-Citation to Create Fake Writing."  
Foundational logic for the mechanical-reuse generators implemented in the Phase 3.3 stress tests.

## 13.3 Comparative Artifacts

**Codex Seraphinianus**  
Serafini, L. (1981). *Codex Seraphinianus*. Milan: Franco Maria Ricci.  
Utilized as the primary modern control for asemic formal writing in Phase 8.

**Lullian Combinatorics**  
Llull, R. (c. 1305). *Ars Magna*.  
Historical reference for the combinatorial wheel systems evaluated as structural relatives in the mechanism identification phase.

**Trithemius Steganography**  
Trithemius, J. (1518). *Polygraphiae*.  
Reference for the disciplined rule-following systems that produce non-semantic text masks, used in Phase 8 case files.

**Lingua Ignota**  
Hildegard von Bingen (c. 1150). *Riesencodex*.  
Used as a boundary control for high-order constructed lexicons without standard grammatical syntax.
