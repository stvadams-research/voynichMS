# Phase 5I Execution Plan (Revised): Mechanism Integrity and External Anchoring

**Project:** Voynich Manuscript, Structural Admissibility Program  
**Phase:** 5I (Revised)  
**Phase Name:** Mechanism Integrity and External Coupling  
**Goal Type:** Validation and scoping of mechanism uniformity under non-textual structure  
**Primary Goal:** Determine whether the already-identified deterministic production mechanism is **globally uniform** across the manuscript or **modulated** by physical manuscript features (sections, layout, illustrations), **without collapsing remaining internal ambiguities** (e.g., DAG vs implicit lattice).

---

## 0. Why This Revision Exists

### 0.1 What changed
The original Phase 5I plan implicitly treated the *Implicit Constraint Lattice* as settled. Subsequent analysis showed that while this topology is a leading candidate, it is **not yet uniquely identified**. Two internal classes remain admissible:

- High-connectivity explicit graph (DAG-like)
- Implicit constraint lattice (feature-conditioned rules)

This revised Phase 5I corrects that framing error.

### 0.2 What this revision does
- Re-scopes Phase 5I to test **mechanism integrity and coupling**, not internal dependency scope.
- Explicitly defers DAG vs lattice discrimination to a later phase (dependency-scope analysis).
- Preserves all previously executed Phase 5I-style analyses as valid, but **reinterprets their conclusions conservatively**.

No data is discarded. Only the claims are tightened.

---

## 1. Phase 5I Purpose and Core Question

### 1.1 Core question
Is the deterministic production mechanism **single and globally stable**, or does it vary in structure or parameters as a function of physical manuscript features such as sections, layout, or illustration proximity?

### 1.2 What Phase 5I tests
- Global versus sectional mechanism stability
- Presence or absence of hybrid or multi-algorithm production
- Whether non-textual features bias or reseed traversal behavior

### 1.3 What Phase 5I explicitly does NOT test
- Where constraints live internally (local edges vs global features)
- Whether the topology is a DAG or an implicit lattice
- Any semantic, symbolic, or interpretive meaning

Those questions are deferred.

---

## 2. Phase 5I Success Criteria

Phase 5I is successful if it produces:

- Evidence that the manuscript is generated by **one global deterministic rule-system**
- Quantification of how invariant key mechanism signatures are across sections
- A clear determination of whether physical manuscript features:
  - change the mechanism,
  - modulate traversal parameters, or
  - have no detectable effect

Failure to find coupling is a valid and informative outcome.

---

## 3. Design Principles

### 3.1 Integrity before refinement
Before refining internal structure, we must establish that there is only **one** structure.

### 3.2 Anchoring without interpretation
Illustrations and layout are treated as **geometric or positional anchors only**, never as carriers of meaning.

### 3.3 Conservative inference
Any result compatible with multiple internal models must be reported as such.

---

## 4. Signature Test Families

### 4.1 Sectional Stability Test (Global Machine Audit)
**Objective:**  
Test whether core mechanism signatures remain stable across codicological sections.

**Metrics:**
- Effective rank (latent dimensionality)
- Successor consistency (path forcing)
- Reset behavior at line boundaries

**Kill rule:**  
If any section exhibits a statistically distinct regime (e.g., different rank scale or successor forcing collapse), the single-mechanism hypothesis is falsified.

---

### 4.2 Cross-Section Structure Sharing
**Objective:**  
Determine whether different sections traverse the **same underlying structure**.

**Metrics:**
- Shared n-gram or path-fragment counts
- Overlap significance relative to matched non-semantic controls

**Interpretation rule:**  
Shared fragments support a common structure but do not imply identical traversal parameters.

---

### 4.3 Proximity-Conditioned Consistency (Exploratory)
**Objective:**  
Test whether proximity to illustrations or layout features modulates traversal determinism.

**Metrics:**
- Successor consistency near anchors versus block text
- Local reset or diversification signatures

**Status:**  
Exploratory only unless adequately powered.

**Non-claim:**  
Absence or presence of coupling does not identify internal constraint scope.

---

## 5. Execution Order

1) **Sectional Profiling**
   - Apply Phase 5B and 5E metrics independently to major sections.
2) **Structure Sharing Analysis**
   - Measure cross-section fragment reuse and overlap significance.
3) **Anchor Proximity Analysis**
   - Test for modulation of successor forcing near physical anchors.
4) **Synthesis**
   - Classify results as:
     - Global uniform
     - Globally uniform with parameter modulation
     - Hybrid (if falsified)

---

## 6. Allowed Conclusions

Phase 5I may conclude:

- Whether the manuscript is generated by a single global mechanism
- Whether sections differ by traversal rather than by rules
- Whether physical structure measurably couples to traversal behavior

Phase 5I may NOT conclude:

- That the mechanism is definitively an implicit lattice
- That the mechanism is definitively a DAG
- That illustrations encode meaning or keys

---

## 7. Deferred Questions (Explicit)

The following are explicitly deferred beyond Phase 5I:

- Constraint locality versus global feature dependence
- DAG versus lattice discrimination
- Any semantic or historical interpretation

These are addressed in the **dependency-scope phase** that follows.
